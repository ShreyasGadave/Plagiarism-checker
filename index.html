Frequently Asked Questions

While writing your paper, you notice a familiar line. Did you read it somewhere? If you borrowed a few lines and didn’t cite them, does that count as plagiarism, even if they’re just a small part?

"Using someone else’s text without attribution is plagiarism, intentional or not. Unintentional plagiarism, even if it's just a sentence, can lead to serious consequences like failing grades. Fortunately, you can ensure your work's authenticity with Writerwise. 1. Direct Plagiarism: This involves copying someone else's work word-for-word without any citation. It's considered the most blatant form of plagiarism and can lead to severe penalties.

2. Self-Plagiarism: This occurs when an author reuses their own previously submitted work in a new context without permission or proper citation. It misleads readers into thinking the work is original.

3. Mosaic Plagiarism: This type involves mixing phrases or ideas from different sources into a new piece without proper attribution. Even if the wording is changed, the original sources must still be credited.

4. Paraphrasing Plagiarism: This occurs when someone rewrites someone else's ideas in their own words but fails to cite the original source. While the wording may differ, the underlying concept is still borrowed.

5. Accidental Plagiarism: This type happens when a writer unintentionally fails to cite sources or misquotes, leading to the unintentional use of another's work. Despite the lack of intent, it still violates academic integrity.

Understanding the different forms of plagiarism is crucial to preventing them. To avoid plagiarism:

1. Always provide proper citations for any ideas, quotes, or information taken from other sources.

2. Utilize plagiarism detection tools like Writerwise to check for unintentional plagiarism before submitting your work.

3. Paraphrase correctly: when restating someone else's ideas, do so in your own words and always cite the original source.

4. Keep track of your sources and maintain a bibliography or works cited page.

Plagiarism involves using someone else’s work or ideas without attribution, regardless of whether the content is protected by copyright. It is primarily an ethical concern, often leading to academic consequences.

Copyright infringement, however, is a legal issue that occurs when copyrighted material is used without permission from the copyright holder. Unlike plagiarism, copyright infringement can result in legal action.
